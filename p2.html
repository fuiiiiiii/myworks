ABOUT

LEICHI RESTAURANT
brand style guide, website redesign
Leichi is a Japanese Izakaya Restaurant located in Santa Clara, California. This restaurant’s branding was to provide customers healthy and delicious food. All of their ingredients are made from scratch and organic. They have explored new ways of combining ingredients on top of the traditional style. However, their logo and website are outdated and inconsistent with their market positioning and pricing.

Project Goal.

The goal of this project is to rebrand the restaurant by creating a Brand Style Guide which consists of research, logo redesign, and mockup demonstrations.

General Research.

About Leichi
Leichi is a Japanese Izakaya Restaurant that provides delicious, healthy and exquisite foods. They have a low-profile on marketing and social media
Environment
Leichi is furnished with dark wood tables and chairs, and it’s overall lighting is darker than average restaurant. The ambient noise is low, makes it a good place to eat, chat, and relax.
Capacity
It is a small restaurant that has the capacity to serve 15-20 customers maximum at a time.

Color Research.

I have made some in-depth research about color theories, and found out that in human eyes, our photo receptors are particularly sensitive to long wavelength light, which we see as RED.
There are hundreds and thousands of successful food companies that use red as their primary color in the logo design. A great example would be McDonald’s, however, to differentiate Leichi from fast foods, I reduced the level of redness and turned the primary colors into orange and orange-like red.

Color Research.

The orange and orange-like red brings up appetites and calls out for attention on top of its overall dark theme of the restaurant interiors.
The black sets a steady tone for its traditional foundation and harmonizes with its overall theme.

Website Redesign.

Leichi’s Original Website
Why Redesign?
Refreshing only the menu is not going to bring enough branding value to Leichi. Its website is very outdated and the fonts are difficult to read.
Problems:
  • Too many typefaces impacts readability.
  • Very limited information hierarchy confuses user
  • Menu information is not complete whereas it should be the most important piece of information on the website.
  • Food photos are low in resolution.
  • Overall website design is messy and has no structure.
Solutions:
  • Reselect and eliminate fonts to two.
  • Re-format and redesign menu information using new fonts.
  • Replace photos with higher resolution food photos to showcase their exquisite products, in this case, the foods.
